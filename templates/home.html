Project Overview

HazardWatch Rwanda is an innovative project designed to strengthen community resilience and disaster risk management in Rwanda. The project directly addresses the increasing challenges posed by climate change, which has led to frequent floods, droughts, and landslides that threaten lives, livelihoods, and infrastructure across the country.

Core Objectives

The primary goal of HazardWatch Rwanda is to provide a comprehensive tool for predicting, monitoring, and responding to climate-related risks. Hazard zones are mapped and shared with policymakers and communities.

Predictive analytics forecast risks such as floods, droughts, and landslides. Alerts are sent via SMS and mobile notifications, reaching even remote areas.

Early warnings empower communities to take timely action, reducing potential loss of life and property. The system also supports long-term disaster preparedness.

Authorities and community organizations can use the data to develop targeted risk-reduction strategies and emergency plans. Local knowledge is integrated into disaster management.

HazardWatch Rwanda builds a safer, more resilient society. Combining technology with community-based planning strengthens local capacity to respond effectively.

This initiative reflects Rwanda’s commitment to sustainable development and climate resilience, offering a scalable model replicable in other regions facing similar risks.

How It Works

Data Collection

Gathers sensor readings, satellite imagery, and community reports to feed the system.

Predict & Monitor

Uses sensor data, satellite imagery, and forecasts to track hazards.

Visualize Risks

Interactive maps show hazard-prone zones at all administrative levels.

Send Alerts

Delivers early warnings via SMS and mobile notifications.

Empower Communities

Provides checklists, planning resources, and quick actions.

Why It’s Unique

HazardWatch Rwanda is community-centered. Unlike generic hazard dashboards, it tailors alerts and resources to Rwanda’s geography and governance model. It ensures inclusivity with offline-friendly outputs (SMS, printable checklists, exportable reports), so even remote communities can benefit.
